Why I am writing about Shopify fake reviews

I am Ollie Hermans, owner and developer of autoBlogger, autoLLMs, autoSchema, autoLock, autoShip, autoStockist, and autoBuy. I have spent years raising concerns about fake Shopify App Store reviews, incentivized reviews, and the way manipulated review signals can distort which apps merchants trust.

This has never been about knocking honest competition. It has been about fairness. Real merchants should be able to compare Shopify apps using real feedback, and developers who build properly should not be pushed down by inflated or artificial review activity.

That is why Shopify's announcement feels important. It is the first clear movement in the direction many of us in the developer community have been asking for.

What Shopify has announced

Shopify has announced two updates aimed at strengthening trust in App Store reviews. The first is a dedicated App Store requirement around review incentivization. In plain English, apps should not offer, unlock, or withhold features in exchange for a merchant leaving a review.

The second is stronger fake-review detection. Shopify says it is expanding the signals used to decide whether a review is authentic, and those signals will apply to both existing reviews and new reviews.

  • Apps found incentivizing reviews can lose a significant portion of reviews.
  • Reviews that do not meet Shopify's authenticity bar may be unpublished.
  • The rollout applies to old and new Shopify App Store reviews.
  • Some genuine apps may lose reviews if those reviews are connected to untrustworthy reviewer activity.

Why this feels like real movement

For a few years now, I have been one of the people consistently making noise about this issue, sharing examples, drumming up support, and pushing for the Shopify App Store review system to be taken seriously. A lot of honest developers have felt the same frustration.

Fake reviews and incentivized reviews create an uneven playing field. They make weaker apps look stronger than they are, make honest developers work uphill, and make merchants spend more time trying to judge which reviews are real.

So yes, I am genuinely excited to see Shopify mention stronger enforcement publicly. This is exactly the kind of movement the developer community has been hoping for.

What honest review collection should look like

The right way to ask for Shopify app reviews is simple: ask real users at the right moment, use neutral language, and never offer anything in return. A review should be feedback, not a transaction.

Negative reviews should not be buried, bought away, or drowned out. They should be answered properly. Merchants notice when developers engage with feedback in a useful way, and that kind of trust is worth more than a polished review count.

  • Ask after a merchant has actually used the app.
  • Keep the review request neutral and optional.
  • Do not unlock features, discounts, credits, or support in exchange for a review.
  • Reply to criticism with context, fixes, or a real support path.

What I hope happens next

I hope this leads to a Shopify App Store where the best apps really can win. Not the loudest apps, not the most manipulated listings, and not the apps with the most questionable review patterns.

The rollout will be worth watching closely. Review counts may change, some removals may be noisy, and Shopify will need to keep refining the signals. But if the end result is a review system merchants can trust more, that is a win for the whole ecosystem.

For my part, I will keep building the autoBlogger app family the right way: useful products, real support, honest feedback, and a long-term focus on helping Shopify merchants grow with better content, clearer data, and more trustworthy tools.
